      Laurel J. Barham is an award winning actress and director, with a passion for inspiring youth, an addiction for telling impactful stories, and a soft spot for rescuing the occasional stray (not always canine). She’s been heavily involved in Acting, Directing and Stage Managing for over 20 years. Laurel started her career teaching public school where she was awarded Teacher of the Year for Alpine School District in ’93 and was a Disney’s American Teacher Nominee in ’97. From there she went on to found her own youth theater program Acting Up! for which she was awarded the SCERA Star Award for enriching the lives of youth in the community through the arts. Acting Up! attended the Musical Theater Competitions of America and won sweepstakes as well as many 1st, 2nd and 3rd place rankings for individual scenes for 3 years running. She has studied at Broadway’s Teaching Lab in NYC and apprenticed Utah’s Legendary Syd Riggs for several years. She’s directed such classics as Of Mice and Men, Crucible, Children of Eden, Boys Next Door and Curious Savage to name a few. She’s taught at the SCERA youth theater, Hale Center Theater and was the Artist in residence at Orem High School. Her stage directing work includes: Hale Center Theatre- Orem, SCERA Shell, SCERA Encore, The Ragan Theatre, The Grove Theatre and most recently at the Ruskin Group Theatre Cafe Plays Series. Laurel's film directing credits include: "Bugged", "Psyched" and the upcoming "I am Woman", a new docuseries soon to be available on YouTube.
